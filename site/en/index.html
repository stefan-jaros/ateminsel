Why breath therapy?

How we breathe reflects how we feel. Stress, anxiety or exhaustion change our breathing, often without us noticing. Breath therapy makes this tangible and opens a path to change.

Middendorf breath therapy works without breathing exercises in the traditional sense. The aim is not to control the breath, but to perceive and allow it.

A film by the Swiss Breath Therapy Association

Breathing difficulties

When breathing becomes shallow, uneven or no longer feels natural, breath therapy helps you find your own rhythm again.

Panic attacks

Fear and panic directly affect how we breathe. Breath therapy strengthens the ability to regulate yourself again.

Stress and burnout

Living under constant pressure changes how we breathe. Breath therapy creates access to calm that thinking alone cannot reach.

Chronic conditions

For asthma, COPD or other chronic conditions, breath therapy can meaningfully complement treatment and ease the way you live with the illness.

Rehabilitation

After surgery, an accident or a prolonged illness, breath therapy supports the body in rebuilding trust and confidence.

Tension

Many tensions are connected to how we breathe. Through the breath, patterns of tension can be recognised and changed.

High sensitivity

Those who perceive a lot need good ways to handle it. Breath therapy helps to use your own sensitivity as a resource.

Mindfulness

To feel the breath is to arrive in the present moment. Breath therapy connects body awareness and mindfulness in a very natural way.
